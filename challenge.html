Bike Theft Mapper

Purpose

This application was created as part of our term project for Geography 307: Socio-Economic Applications of GIS at McGill University, submitted on April 17, 2015.

Volunteered Geographic Information (VGI) is an increasingly popular method of data collection that is based on information provided willingly by individuals. Sometimes seen as a democratization of data, VGI based processes tend to result in end products that are more beneficial and accessible to users â€“ a possible factor in their motivation to contribute. For this project we sought to facilitate the generation and display of VGI data for bike thefts in Montreal as well as to make pre-existing bike theft data more accessible to the average user.

We aimed to create a live mapping platform that showed past thefts and allowed the contribution of additional data, to create an evolving map of bike thefts in Montreal (and scalable to the whole world). Through this we hoped to provide users with way to view where past thefts have occurred, so that they might gauge the threat of having their bike, or part of their bike, stolen in a particular neighbourhood. To further increase the utility of the platform we also identified bike theft hot spots by census tract, and analyzed the relative likelihood of theft in the area surrounding bike paths.

For more information on our research goals and methodology, you may view the research paper we submitted for the project here.
